A Reading Guide

East and West

Resources for understanding, and helping to heal, the millennium-old division between Catholic and Orthodox Christians — the heart of the Foundation’s work as the Church looks toward 2054.

The estrangement between Catholic and Orthodox Christians is the oldest open wound in the Body of Christ, and the Foundation exists in large part to help close it before the schism of 1054 reaches its thousandth year. This guide gathers the books and resources we would put in a reader’s hands first: the works of James Likoudis, himself a convert from Greek Orthodoxy, the essential modern scholarship on both sides of the questions, the Church’s own words, and the living institutions where the dialogue continues.

The Works of James Likoudis

James Likoudis devoted his life to the cause of reunion, and his books on the question remain the most accessible Catholic treatment of the issues. The first has just returned to print, and the thematic collections that follow, compiled and edited by Andrew Likoudis, gather the harvest of his seven decades of labor.

Ending the Byzantine Greek Schism

James Likoudis · Third Edition, Emmaus Road · Foreword by Scott Hahn

The definitive Catholic treatment of the theological issues dividing East and West, newly returned to print in a third revised edition. Alongside the author’s own analysis of papal primacy and the Filioque, it gathers the medieval sources themselves: the fourteenth-century Apologia of Demetrios Kydones for unity with Rome and St. Thomas Aquinas’s Contra Errores Graecorum.

The Divine Primacy of the Bishop of Rome and Modern Eastern Orthodoxy

James Likoudis

Written as letters to a Greek Orthodox inquirer on the unity of the Church, a patient case for the primacy of the Bishop of Rome that answers the objections of Orthodox and Protestant critics point by point.

Find the Book ›

Eastern Orthodoxy and the See of Peter

James Likoudis

The third panel of the trilogy, examining the witness of the Christian East itself to the Petrine ministry, from an author whose own journey led from Greek Orthodoxy into full communion with Rome.

Find the Book ›

The Divine Mosaic: Piecing Together Catholic and Orthodox Unity

James Likoudis · Edited by Andrew Likoudis · Foreword by Tim Staples

Essays, speeches, and presentations from seven decades of labor for reunion, gathered into a single volume. Among much else it includes his critical review of Siecienski’s scholarship, a Catholic answer to the Orthodox volumes recommended below.

Find the Book ›

Heralds of a Catholic Russia

James Likoudis · Blue Army Press, 2023

Essays on the Russian thinkers and believers who found their way to Rome, and on the enduring hope, bound by Our Lady of Fatima to Russia by name, of that nation’s reconciliation with the See of Peter.

View Details ›

Unpacking Palamism

James Likoudis · Compiled & Edited by Andrew Likoudis · En Route, 2024

His essays on Gregory Palamas and the essence–energies controversy, the fourteenth-century dispute that remains among the deepest theological differences between East and West, gathered into a single volume.

View Details ›

Scholars of the Sacred: Dominican Theologians in Late Medieval Byzantium

James Likoudis · Compiled & Edited by Andrew Likoudis · En Route, 2023

Essays on the Dominicans of the late medieval Greek East and the Byzantine minds around them, the world of the Kydones brothers, where Thomism first met the East on its own soil; the tradition from which the Foundation’s own journal takes its name.

View Details ›
The Essential Scholarship

Five modern studies that map what is actually in dispute and how the estrangement happened. Note that Siecienski writes as an Orthodox scholar; Congar, DeVille, and Nichols are Catholics, and James Likoudis’s critique of Siecienski appears in The Divine Mosaic above.

The Filioque: History of a Doctrinal Controversy

A. Edward Siecienski · An Orthodox Author · Oxford University Press

The standard scholarly history of the dispute over the procession of the Holy Spirit, traced from the Fathers to the present. Siecienski, an Orthodox historian, is widely credited with fairness to both traditions, though his framing of the disputed questions is his own.

Find the Book ›

The Papacy and the Orthodox: Sources and History of a Debate

A. Edward Siecienski · An Orthodox Author · Oxford University Press

The companion volume on Roman primacy, working through the sources from Peter’s ministry to the modern dialogues; a valuable map of where the real disagreements lie, read with the awareness that its author writes from the Orthodox side of them.

Find the Book ›

Orthodoxy and the Roman Papacy: Ut Unum Sint and the Prospects of East–West Unity

Adam A. J. DeVille · University of Notre Dame Press

Takes up John Paul II’s invitation to imagine how the papacy might be exercised in a reunited Church, gathering Orthodox critiques and proposals and answering them with concrete structural suggestions.

Find the Book ›

After Nine Hundred Years

Yves Congar, OP

Congar’s classic study of the background of the schism, showing how centuries of cultural, political, and psychological drift, as much as any doctrine, made strangers of brothers.

Find the Book ›

Rome and the Eastern Churches: A Study in Schism

Aidan Nichols, OP · Ignatius Press

A sweeping Catholic history of the separations between Rome and the Christian East, from the early Christological divisions through 1054 and beyond, with a sober assessment of what reunion would actually require.

Find the Book ›
Orthodoxy on Its Own Terms

Reunion requires understanding the other as he understands himself. The following is an Orthodox work by an Orthodox author, recommended here for exactly that purpose, as a window into how Orthodoxy presents itself, not as a statement of Catholic teaching.

The Orthodox Church

Metropolitan Kallistos (Timothy) Ware · An Orthodox Author · Penguin

The classic introduction to Orthodox history, theology, and worship, written from within the Orthodox Church by the Oxford scholar and Orthodox metropolitan who was a longtime friend of the Orientale Lumen conferences. Readers should note its accounts of the disputed questions are Orthodox ones.

Find the Book ›
From the Magisterium and the Dialogue

The Church’s own words come first, and all of these documents are freely available online.

Orientalium Ecclesiarum

Second Vatican Council, 1964

The Council’s decree on the Eastern Catholic Churches, affirming that their venerable traditions of theology, liturgy, and discipline belong to the full catholicity of the Church and are to be preserved, not latinized.

Ut Unum Sint

John Paul II, 1995

The great encyclical on Christian unity, in which the pope invited other Christians to help him find a way of exercising the Petrine ministry open to a new situation, the invitation to which much of the modern dialogue responds.

Orientale Lumen

John Paul II, 1995

The apostolic letter on the treasures of the Christian East, issued the same month, from which the Foundation’s partner conferences take their name; the Church, the pope wrote, must learn again to breathe with both her lungs.

The Bishop of Rome

Dicastery for Promoting Christian Unity, 2024

The study document synthesizing thirty years of responses to Ut Unum Sint and fifty ecumenical dialogues on primacy and synodality, published with the approval of Pope Francis, and concluding with practical suggestions toward an exercise of primacy in the twenty-first century.

The Ravenna Document

Joint International Commission · 2007

Ecclesiological and Canonical Consequences of the Sacramental Nature of the Church: the agreed statement in which Catholics and Orthodox together affirmed that at every level of the Church’s life there is both synodality and primacy, and that at the universal level the protos is the Bishop of Rome according to the ancient order of the churches.

Read the Document ›

The Chieti Document

Joint International Commission · 2016

Synodality and Primacy During the First Millennium: the commission’s common reading of how authority actually worked in the undivided Church, offered as a shared foundation for resolving what divides the second millennium.

Read the Document ›

The Alexandria Document

Joint International Commission · 2023

Synodality and Primacy in the Second Millennium and Today: the most recent agreed statement, which extends the work of Ravenna and Chieti into the divided millennium itself. It concludes, in the commission’s own words, that the Church is ‘not properly understood as a pyramid’ nor as ‘a federation of self-sufficient Churches.’

Read the Document ›
The Ongoing Work

The dialogue is not only a shelf of books; it is a living work carried on by institutions anyone can follow and support.

The Kydones Review

Likoudis Legacy Foundation

The Foundation’s own journal of Catholic–Orthodox scholarship, named for the fourteenth-century Byzantine statesman who translated Aquinas into Greek and made the case for unity from within the East.

The Orientale Lumen Conferences

olfoundation.net

Annual gatherings of Catholic and Orthodox hierarchs, scholars, and laity founded by Jack Figel in 1997, and the only lay ecumenical movement of its kind blessed by bishops and patriarchs of both churches. The Foundation helped organize the thirtieth conference this July at the St. John Paul II Shrine in Washington.

The Society of Saint John Chrysostom

Founded 1926

An ecumenical association of Catholic and Orthodox clergy and laity promoting the understanding of Eastern Christianity and praying for the unity of East and West, at work in England since 1926 and in the United States since 1998, and a principal sponsor of the Orientale Lumen conferences from their beginning.

The North American Orthodox–Catholic Theological Consultation

Since 1965

The oldest continuing official dialogue between the two churches anywhere in the world, whose agreed statements over six decades are a quiet record of how much common ground has already been found.

The Foundation’s work is oriented toward a single hope: that the second millennium of the schism, which begins in 2054, will never arrive. Please share this guide with anyone drawn to that hope, and pray for the unity of East and West.
