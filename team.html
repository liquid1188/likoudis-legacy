Dr. James Likoudis
(1928–2024)

A convert to the Catholic Church in 1952 from Eastern Orthodoxy, James Likoudis devoted over seventy years to a single project: the scholarly and pastoral case for Catholic-Orthodox reunion. He is regarded as a leading authority on Ecumenism, the Papacy, Catechetics, and the role of the Laity in the Church.

He authored more than ten books and over 300 scholarly essays, and was awarded an honorary doctorate of divinity from Sacred Heart Major Seminary in 2020. He was married to his wife Ruth for 71 years and is the father of six, with thirty-five grandchildren and forty-five great-grandchildren.

Board of Directors

Andrew Likoudis, MA

Chairman & President

A Catholic writer, editor, and consultant based in Baltimore. As founder and chairman of the Likoudis Legacy Foundation, he carries forward the ecumenical and apologetic legacy of his grandfather, the late theologian James Likoudis. He serves as editor-in-chief of The Kydones Review and is completing an M.A. in Catholic Studies at Franciscan University of Steubenville. He is a contributor to the National Catholic Register, Where Peter Is, EWTN News, and Philosophy Now, and a fellow of Goldman Sachs 10,000 Small Businesses and Johns Hopkins University.

  • Editor, Faith in Crisis — foreword by Rocco Buttiglione (En Route Books, 2024)
  • Editor-in-Chief, The Kydones Review
  • Contributor: National Catholic Register, Where Peter Is, EWTN News, Philosophy Now

Philip Blosser, PhD

Born in China and raised in Japan, Dr. Blosser earned his BA from Sophia University in Tokyo and his PhD from Duquesne University. He is the author of Scheler's Critique of Kant's Ethics and blogs at Musings of a Pertinacious Papist. He has taught philosophy at Sacred Heart Major Seminary in Detroit for many years and serves as Secretary of the Max Scheler Society of North America.

  • Professor of Philosophy, Sacred Heart Major Seminary
  • Secretary, Max Scheler Society of North America

Mike Aquilina

Co-founder of the St. Paul Center for Biblical Theology with Scott and Kimberly Hahn. He has hosted eleven television series on EWTN and edited New Covenant magazine. A songwriter whose work has been recorded by numerous Catholic artists, he is one of the most prolific and widely read authors in contemporary Catholic letters, with over seventy books to his name.

  • Executive Vice President, St. Paul Center for Biblical Theology
  • Author of over 70 books on Catholic history and devotion
  • Contributing editor, Angelus News

Will Deatherage, MA

Deatherage holds an M.A. in Historical-Systematic Theology from The Catholic University of America. He specializes in digital marketing and development for Catholic organizations and publishes theological content through Clarifying Catholicism. He serves as CEO of Catholics for Hire LLC and Executive Director of The New Realist Project.

  • CEO, Catholics for Hire LLC
  • Executive Director, Clarifying Catholicism
  • Executive Director, The New Realist Project

Robert Fastiggi, PhD

Dr. Fastiggi holds an M.A. and Ph.D. from Fordham University and has taught at Sacred Heart Major Seminary since 1999. He co-edited the English translation of the 43rd edition of Denzinger-Hünermann (Ignatius Press, 2012), the standard reference work of Catholic doctrinal sources, and is President Emeritus of the Mariological Society of America.

  • Bishop Kevin M. Britt Chair of Dogmatic Theology, Sacred Heart Major Seminary
  • Co-editor, Denzinger-Hünermann 43rd ed. (Ignatius Press, 2012)
  • President Emeritus, Mariological Society of America

Stephen Krason, PhD, JD

Dr. Krason earned his J.D. and Ph.D. in Political Science at SUNY Buffalo and an M.A. in Theology from Gannon University. He is admitted to the bar of the U.S. Supreme Court. In 1992 he co-founded the Society of Catholic Social Scientists, of which he is President and Chairman, and has been Professor of Political Science and Legal Studies at Franciscan University of Steubenville for over three decades.

  • Professor Emeritus of Political Science and Legal Studies, Franciscan University of Steubenville
  • Co-Founder, President & Chairman, Society of Catholic Social Scientists
  • Admitted to the bar of the U.S. Supreme Court

Jack Figel

A devoted layman-ecumenist with decades of engagement in Catholic-Orthodox dialogue. He founded Eastern Christian Publications in 1993 and has chaired the Orientale Lumen Foundation since its inception, organizing the annual Orientale Lumen conferences that bring together Eastern and Western Christians for theological dialogue.

  • Publisher, Eastern Christian Publications (Fairfax, VA)
  • Chairman, Orientale Lumen Foundation and Conferences
  • Sponsor, Figel Event for Ecumenism — Washington Theological Consortium
