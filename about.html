About the Foundation

Advancing Ecumenical Scholarship & Christian Unity

Our Mission

A Foundation for the Long Work of Unity

The Likoudis Legacy Foundation is a 501(c)(3) nonprofit research institute dedicated to advancing ecumenical theology, interreligious dialogue, and the lay apostolate. We were founded in 2023 to honor and extend the intellectual legacy of Dr. James Likoudis — one of the most consequential American Catholic lay theologians of the twentieth century.

Likoudis devoted more than seventy years to a single, coherent project: the reunion of the Eastern Orthodox and Catholic Churches. He argued, documented, and testified to the possibility of that reunion — through books, essays, lectures, and decades of correspondence with Orthodox clergy and scholars. His work helped define what serious ecumenism looked like at the level of the laity.

The Foundation carries that project forward through scholarship, publication, education, and institutional bridge-building. We are directed by Andrew Likoudis, James's grandson, and governed by a board of theologians, academics, and Catholic intellectuals committed to the magisterial center.

Meet the Team

I.

Ecumenical & Interreligious Dialogue

Supporting serious theological engagement with separated Christians and other religious traditions — beginning with Catholic-Orthodox reunion, which animated James Likoudis's entire life's work, and extending to the wider interreligious horizon the Church has embraced since Vatican II. Dialogue that names real differences clearly enough that they might one day be resolved.

II.

Defense of the Magisterial Center

Standing for Vatican II read in continuity, against both progressive reinterpretation and traditionalist rupture. The via media is harder to hold than its alternatives — which is precisely why it needs to be held.

III.

The Lay Apostolate

James Likoudis did his life's work as a layman, a father, and a husband. The Foundation honors that witness — that deep theological formation and serious Christian engagement are not the exclusive preserve of the ordained.

Our Patron

Dr. James Likoudis
(1928–2024)

James Likoudis was born in 1928 in Lackawanna, New York, to a Greek Orthodox family. He converted to Catholicism in 1952 and spent the next seven decades pursuing a single conviction: that the reunion of Eastern and Western Christendom was not merely possible, but necessary — and that it demanded honest, patient, patristically grounded argument rather than diplomatic vagueness.

He wrote more than ten books and over 300 scholarly essays, was awarded an honorary doctorate of divinity from Sacred Heart Major Seminary in 2020, and was recognized by Catholic leaders including Cardinal Burke and Cardinal Sarah. His work on the papacy, the filioque, Mariology, and Catholic moral teaching appeared in dozens of journals and was acquired by the world's premier research libraries — from Oxford and Harvard to the Library of Congress.

He died in September 2024, having been married to his wife Ruth for 71 years. He was 95.

Our Programs

The Foundation operates across publication, education, translation, and institutional dialogue — each program rooted in the same conviction that drove James Likoudis: that good scholarship serves the Church.

Our Reach

A Distinguished Network

The Foundation's work is supported by an esteemed network of bishops, cardinals, seminary professors, and research fellows from premier institutions. James Likoudis was endorsed by figures including Raymond Cardinal Burke, the late Alice von Hildebrand, and Dr. Scott Hahn. His books are held in the collections of Oxford, Harvard, Princeton, the British Library, the Library of Congress, and dozens of Catholic seminaries worldwide.

The Foundation's board and advisory council include leading scholars in dogmatic theology, philosophy, and Catholic social science — drawn from Sacred Heart Major Seminary, Franciscan University of Steubenville, and beyond.
