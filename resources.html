A Reader's Guide

Where to Turn

Trustworthy books and sources for navigating the crisis of traditionalism and the SSPX — for the confused, the grieving, and those watching someone they love drift toward schism.

Since the consecrations at Écône and the excommunications that followed, we have heard from a good number of readers who are unsettled — some grieving over family and friends, some fielding hard questions in their parishes, and some simply unsure where to begin reading. This guide is our attempt at an answer: the short shelf of books we would put in someone's hands first, along with a few sources worth following as events unfold.

A schism is a sorrow before it is anything else, and the first thing owed to those who have been drawn away is our grief and our prayer. Still, many people caught in the middle of this moment need something solid to read, and it is for them that this guide exists.

Start Here

If you read nothing else, read these three, which between them cover what the Council actually taught, why the liturgy was reformed, and how one of the great theologians of the twentieth century judged the Lefebvre affair while it was still unfolding.

Challenge to the Church: The Case of Archbishop Lefebvre

Yves Congar, OP · 50th-Anniversary Edition, Edited by Andrew Likoudis

Congar, whose work shaped the Second Vatican Council, examined the Lefebvre crisis as it broke in 1976, and his judgment has lost none of its force in the fifty years since. We reissued it this month for precisely this moment.

Find the Book ›

The Pope, the Council, and the Mass

James Likoudis & Kenneth D. Whitehead · Revised Edition

James Likoudis's classic, and for decades the standard first reference on the objections traditionalists raise about the Council and the reformed liturgy, worked through question by question with the documents in hand — and praised in its day by L'Osservatore Romano and Fr. John Hardon.

Find the Book ›

Faith in Crisis: Critical Dialogues in Catholic Traditionalism, Church Authority, and Reform

Edited by Andrew Likoudis · Foreword by Rocco Buttiglione

Essays from a range of scholars on authority, tradition, and reform, gathered so that a reader can think these questions through with the whole picture in view.

Find the Book ›
A Life Inside It

Arguments only carry a person so far, and sometimes what finally reaches someone is the story of a man who lived it and found his way out.

Traddyland: Memoir of a Radical Traditional Catholic

Louis Massett

Massett grew up inside the SSPX world of St. Mary's, Kansas, and his memoir tells what that world was like from within and what it cost him to find his way back into full communion.

Find the Book ›
Understanding Traditionalism

These three take the movement's arguments seriously enough to answer them properly, and two of their authors write from long experience inside it.

Altar Against Altar: An Analysis of Catholic Traditionalism

Andrew Mioni · Foreword by Richard DeClue

Mioni writes from years inside these circles, and he works patiently through the "state of necessity" and "supplied jurisdiction" claims on which the independent ministries rest until very little is left standing of either.

Find the Book ›

Heresy Disguised as Tradition

Pedro Gabriel

Gabriel shows that movements claiming a purer tradition against the living Magisterium are nothing new in the Church's history, and that today's radical traditionalism repeats their pattern almost point for point.

Find the Book ›

Rigidity: Faithfulness or Heterodoxy?

Pedro Gabriel

A study of what the Church actually asks of the faithful, and of why rigidity should never be mistaken for fidelity.

Find the Book ›
The Deeper Story

The crisis of faith did not begin in 1965, and these two books trace it to its real roots, reclaiming along the way a saint the movement has wrongly claimed for its own.

The Unknown Modern Side of St. Pius X

Pedro Gabriel · Foreword by Robert Fastiggi

The pope so often invoked against the modern Church was in fact a reformer, and Gabriel shows how much of his liturgical and pastoral work anticipated the very developments traditionalists now resist in his name.

Find the Book ›

Ignorance of Things Divine: The True Crisis of Faith — And Its Remedy — According to the Magisterium

Andrew Mioni

Traces the crisis of faith through two and a half centuries of papal teaching and shows that its roots run far deeper than the Second Vatican Council.

Find the Book ›

Where to Read the News

A few outlets we trust for reporting on the Church, with a plain word on where each stands editorially.

Where Peter Is

wherepeteris.com

Reporting and commentary faithful to the Magisterium, which is the site's whole mission.

Vatican News

vaticannews.va

The Holy See's own news service, and the place to read what Rome has actually said before reading what anyone else says about it.

OSV News

osvnews.com

The national Catholic news service, widely syndicated across diocesan papers and websites.

Word on Fire

wordonfire.org

Bishop Robert Barron's Evangelization & Culture journal, with much of its writing available online.

National Catholic Register

ncregister.com

Long-established national reporting and analysis; its editorial outlook leans to the conservative side.

The Pillar

pillarcatholic.com

Sharp investigative journalism on the life and governance of the Church; likewise leans to the conservative side.

Commonweal

commonwealmagazine.org

A lay-edited review of religion, politics, and culture, written from a more progressive editorial perspective.

The Tablet

thetablet.co.uk

The British Catholic weekly — international in scope, and likewise more progressive in tone.

The Church has weathered worse than this, and the promise made to her has never failed. Please feel free to share this guide with anyone it might help, and keep in your prayers all who have been led out of communion in these weeks, praying especially that they find their way home.
